'Parents Without Partners' Site Redesign

Support for single parents all over the country.

My Role:

Researcher, Designer (Team: 4 members)

Duration:

3 weeks

Platform:

Desktop

Tools:

Figma, Miro, InVision, Google Docs/ Spreadsheets /Forms, Trello

Overview

Our project challenge was to redesign a nonprofit organization website of our choosing. Together, my group chose the 'Parents Without Partners' nonprofit organization.

Goal

In order to keep up with competitors and to improve the overall mood of the site, our group completely redesigned the styling, navigation, information and resources offered on the site.

The Problem

Single parents can quickly feel overwhelmed, stressed and lost due to the amount of responsibility they have to shoulder. However, it's difficult for them to find the resources they need and support from people who truly understand the situation they're in.

The Solution

Single parents deserve to be able to find the resources they need and support from people who truly understand the situation they're in. Parents Without Partners provides single parents with a support network of other single parents to trade stories, struggles and advice.

Research

Our research started with attempts to contact stakeholders at Parents Without Partners. However, when we weren’t able to get a response, we turned to single parents to gather a better understanding of their personal situations and needs. We interviewed 4 single parents, seeking to understand three things:

  • Their current situation

  • Whether or not they would take advantage of a support group offered strictly for single parents

  • What (if any) other resources or information would be beneficial for them to have access to

Key Findings

  • A significant difference between generations: an older single parent whose children are now adults doesn’t consider being a single parent 2 decades ago as difficult as a single parent with a 2-year old now

  • Single parents nowadays would like more advice and resources to help them without having to go on a wild goose chase to find them

  • Many of them don’t have the free time to search for the things they need

  • Feel highly stressed and without a support network of people that understand their situation

  • They feel frustrated and alone

Our conclusion drawn by our research was that Parents Without Partners is a resource many single parents would take advantage of if they'd heard of it before, but their needs go beyond having just the support of single parents in similar situations. They want guidance in the home and through legal struggles, access to news and events, as well as the ability to make friends and connections through a support network.

Competitors

From our research, we identified 2 direct competitors and 2 indirect competitors of Parents Without Partners:

Direct Competitors

  • The Single Parent Project is a nonprofit organization 'dedicated to providing support, stability, and community for Single Parent families.' They aim to help 'working single parents who make too much for government assistance, but not enough to thrive.' They offer a variety of resources, most notabily financial assistance

  • Bridge Of Hope is a Christain nonprofit organization with the mission of ending family homelessness. While any family counts, they primarily focus on assisting those comprised of single mothers and their children.

Indirect Competitors

  • Meetup.com is a website that establishes community based meetups, promiting connections and support groups. Users can create or join a group and can search for events based off of interest or location.

  • Facebook is a social media network that connects a user to other users all over the world. There are many groups comprised of thousands of users that already exists on the site.

User Persona

Meet Cindy Anderson, a 25 year-old single mother to two lovely children, Celeste Anderson (aged 4) and Thomas Anderson (aged 1). She currently works 2 jobs as a waitress and a cashier, and makes around 3k per month. She and her children live in a small apartment in Colombus, Ohio.

Cindy struggles with finding the time between her two jobs to support her children the way they need and deserve. Daycare is extremely expensive and with the rocketing prices of food, housing, education, and transportation, Cindy experiences feelings of being overwhelmed and underprepared.

Pain Points

  • High stress, custody battles

  • Less opportunity for her to be able to spend time with her children

  • Expensive cost of living

  • The entire process has affected the children, making them more emotional. Cindy experiences a huge lack of support from outside resources and friends/family members who want to help but don't understand what she's going through.

Goals

  • Finding emotional and mental support during hard times, accessible resources for childcare, family enrichment, and legal assistance

  • Earn enough to support and care for her children's basic needs

  • Keep her children emotionally and mentally happy

  • Job/career building and assistance to enrich and empower single parents

User Journey Map

We created a user journey map for Cindy in order to understand her emotions and the things that might motivate her to searching for the Parents Without Partners support group. In order to create a positive experience for her, it was important that we extremalise the situation she may find herself in during the end of the year holidays.

User Senario

Holidays are approaching and Cindy is finding herself overwhelmed financially, working double shifts and still unable to pay all the bills. Worse still, she's unable to give her children all that they want and deserve. She becomes exhausted, depressed, and hopeless for the envisioned holiday she wanted to give her children. In an effort to vent and possibly fingure out solutions, she turns to online resources for connections.

Sitemap

Before sketching, our team determined what the new sitemap would be given we were redesigning the entire site. We determined that we would add 3 new pages to the site: a Resources page, a Regions page, and a Join Today page.

Initial Prototypes

Each team member quickly sketched their ideas of what the redesigned site should look like. Our team decided to focus on redesigning the Home page, About Page and Regions Page. We then went through many initial lo-fi prototype versions as we combined our ideas from our initial sketches.

Usability Testing and Findings

Objective

The objective of conducting this user testing is to ensure that users can easily navigate and find relavent information on the completely redesigned prototype.

Target Users

Single parents seeking to join a support group of other single parents, general resources, and events/news in their area.

Tasks to Complete

  1. Sucessfully identify your zone (ex. A, B, C, D, E)

  2. Locate the (placeholder) application page

  3. Return to the Home page

Number of People Tested

4

Key Takeaways

  • Simplify the 'regions' page to become a 'contact us' page

  • Unclear what 'zones' are or mean

  • Enjoy and appreciate the ease of the 'join us' page that also contains FAQs

  • Love the color choice - pink is often associated with pregnancy and parenthood

With our user feedback, we revisited our sitemap and decided to combine the Regions and Contact Us page to become 1 Contact Us page.
